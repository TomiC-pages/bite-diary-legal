Privacy policy for Bite Diary

Last updated: 4 May 2026

This privacy policy explains how Bite Diary ("the App") handles your information. The App is developed and operated by the developer of Bite Diary ("we", "us", or "our"). By installing and using the App you agree to the practices described here.

If you do not agree with this policy, please do not install or use the App.

What information the App collects

Bite Diary stores the following information only on your own device:

  • Daily food entries you log, including food name, serving size, meal type, and date
  • Custom foods you create and their nutritional values
  • Photographs you take of nutrition labels using the in-app scanner
  • Text extracted from nutrition labels by the on-device text recognition
  • Barcode values you have scanned and the nutritional data retrieved for them
  • Your daily calorie and macronutrient goals
  • Your theme preference and other app settings

Bite Diary does not collect:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or other identifying details
  • Your location
  • Your device identifiers, advertising IDs, or any analytics
  • Crash reports or telemetry
  • Any information about other apps installed on your device

Where your data is stored

All data described above is stored locally on your device in an SQLite database and your device's preferences storage. The App does not include account creation, cloud sync, or any backend service we operate. The single exception is the barcode lookup feature described below, which sends only a barcode number to Open Food Facts.

If you uninstall the App, all stored data is deleted along with it. We have no copy.

Photographs and the camera

The App requests permission to use your device's camera and to read images from your gallery. These permissions are used only when you tap the "Scan a nutrition label" button or choose an image from your gallery within that flow.

Photographs you take or select are processed entirely on your device using Google's ML Kit on-device text recognition. The image and the recognized text never leave your device. The image is also not retained after the scan completes; only the parsed nutritional values, which you can edit, are saved as part of the food entry you choose to log.

Barcode scanning and Open Food Facts

The App can scan product barcodes and look up their nutritional information from Open Food Facts (https://world.openfoodfacts.org), a free, open, crowd-sourced food database operated by the non-profit Open Food Facts association.

When you scan a barcode, the App sends only the barcode number itself to Open Food Facts and receives back the product's name and nutritional values. The App does not send any personal data, location data, photographs, or device identifiers to Open Food Facts. A generic User-Agent header identifying the App ("Bite Diary") is included with the request as required by Open Food Facts policy.

Open Food Facts may log incoming requests on their servers as part of standard web-server operation. Open Food Facts has its own privacy policy available at https://world.openfoodfacts.org/privacy. The App developer has no relationship with Open Food Facts beyond using their public API and is not responsible for their policies.

If you choose to contribute a product back to Open Food Facts using the in-app contribution feature (when available), the nutritional values you provide will be uploaded to Open Food Facts' public database. By using that feature you agree to Open Food Facts' contribution terms. Contribution is always optional and you control whether to contribute each product.

Permissions the App requests

The App requests the following Android permissions, each used only for the purpose listed:

  • Camera — to capture photographs of nutrition labels and to scan product barcodes
  • Read media images — to let you pick a previously saved photograph of a nutrition label from your gallery
  • Internet — used for two purposes: (1) for ML Kit to download its on-device text-recognition model the first time you use the label scan feature; (2) to send a barcode number to Open Food Facts and receive nutritional information back when you use the barcode scan feature

The App does not request location, contacts, microphone, calendar, SMS, or any other sensitive permission.

Third-party libraries

The App uses the following third-party software libraries that operate entirely on your device:

  • Google ML Kit Text Recognition — for converting photographs of nutrition labels into text
  • mobile_scanner — for decoding barcodes from camera images on the device
  • openfoodfacts — the official Open Food Facts SDK, used to look up products by barcode
  • sqflite — for the local database
  • Provider — for app state management
  • Material 3 / Flutter — for the user interface
  • fl_chart, percent_indicator, google_fonts, intl — for visual elements

Apart from the barcode-lookup network requests to Open Food Facts described above, none of these libraries transmit your personal data to any external service through the App. The Google ML Kit library will, on first use, download a small text-recognition model from Google's servers; this is a one-time technical download with no association to you as a user.

Children's privacy

The App is not specifically designed for or directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13, because we do not collect information from anyone. If you are a parent or guardian and would like more information about how the App works on a child's device, please contact us using the email below.

Health and medical disclaimer

Bite Diary is a personal calorie and food tracking tool. It is not a medical device, and the information it provides — including nutritional values, calorie goals, and macronutrient breakdowns — is not medical or nutritional advice. The pre-loaded food database draws on publicly available USDA FoodData Central values and may contain inaccuracies. Nutritional values you enter manually, that are extracted from nutrition labels by the scanner, or that are retrieved from Open Food Facts via barcode lookup may be incomplete or wrong. Open Food Facts data is contributed by volunteers and is not authoritative.

Before making significant changes to your diet, especially if you have a medical condition, are pregnant or nursing, are under 18, or are at risk of an eating disorder, consult a qualified healthcare professional. Do not rely on the App as your sole source of nutritional information.

Your choices

You can stop the App from accessing the camera or your photo gallery at any time by revoking those permissions in your Android settings. Doing so will not delete any data you have already saved.

You can delete all data stored by the App by uninstalling it from your device.
